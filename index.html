1930s

The 1930s typographic style is inspired by the French painter and typeface designer Adolphe Jean-Marie Mouron, better known by his pseudonym Cassandre. Though his lifetime spans more than half of the XX century, many of his most remarkable works and creations date back to the 1920s and the 1930s. Here are some references from this period:

Cover page:

Both the size and the font family comply with the instructions provided by Cassandre and his colleagues. As suggested by a Bifur promotional brochure, the Bifur font was specifically designed for headings, while it should not be used for text. The same document also introduces four different sizes: we have used the largest one (60pt) for the main heading of the whole article, while the third size (36pt) was chosen to indicate the author.
